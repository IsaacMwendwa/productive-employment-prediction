Predictive Analysis of Productive Employment Based on Economic Growth in Kenya

Background Information

Productive employment is employment yielding sufficient returns to labour to permit a worker and his/her dependents a level of consumption above the poverty line

The United Nations has set the poverty line to a consumption of below $1.90 a day

Predictive Analysis of Productive Employment

Welcome to do a predictive analysis of productive employment based on economic growth in Kenya. Specifically, we will be predicting the number of people earning below the poverty line (Wage_bracket_0_to_10000), per industry. This group is referred to as the working poor. Moreover, we will also predict the total number of people in employment per industry.

This predictive analytics project seeks to provide actionable insights to drive policy making and action, which will guarantee the attainment of Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) Number 8, which is to "Promote sustained, inclusive and sustainable economic growth, full and productive employment and decent work for all."

The prediction problem is formulated as: Given the economic growth indicators (Contribution by GDP, Growth of GDP) by sector or industry, predict the number of people in non-productive employment (working poor) and the total number of people in employment per sector/industry.
